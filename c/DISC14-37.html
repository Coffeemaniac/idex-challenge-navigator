DISC 14 Indian Navy DISC14-37 PS 37

Problem Statement

Development of advance Rounds Monitoring System for usage onboard ships

Challenge Brief / Definition

Currently, ships use manual way of logging and recording of
Safety Rounds undertaken onboard ships. The individual presently goes to a compartment and signs a book for recording of the visit. Thereafter, the rounds are registered manually for record keeping.
The challenge is to develop a robust, advanced, user friendly
Rounds Monitoring System for recording of the visits. It will be able to record the visit to a particular compartment with a timestamp. The equipment shall be robust against marine environments and shall be able to withstand salinity, humidity or vibration over time.

Existing Solution

(if No
The existing manual way is workforce intensive and time consuming. Additionally, the record keeping does not have time stamp.

Technology Domains

Software and RFID

Application / Use Case

Safety Rounds onboard IN ships

Project Outcome

The expected prototype is envisaged to have advance rounds monitoring System.
The equipment should be robust and strong withstand vibrations etc.

Testing / Certification

To be mutually agreed upon by firm and IN

Potential Market

· The equipment shall be provisioned onboard all IN ships.
· The Indian Coast Guard can also procure the equipment for shipboard usage
· The product will be one of its kind, if developed to requisite standards and quality and may have export potential including use case in merchant marine.

Business Case

The equipment will be provisioned onboard all small ships.
