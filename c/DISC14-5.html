ALS / 2.5 Ton Vehicle Mounted Barrel Handling Swivel Arm

Open in full navigator → Browse all 209 challenges
DISC 14 Indian Army DISC14-5 PS 5

Problem Statement

· Loading and unloading filled barrels currently relies on manual handling, risking injury and delays.
· Existing handling methods are manpower- intensive and inefficient in field and operational conditions.
· A compact, vehicle-mounted mechanical assistance system is required for safe barrel handling.

Challenge Brief / Definition

· Develop a retrofittable swivel arm for ALS and 2.5 Ton vehicles.
· The swivel arm shall assist loading and unloading of fully filled barrels.
· The system shall operate using the vehicle’s existing engine power.
· Fitment shall not increase overall vehicle dimensions or compromise mobility.

Existing Solution

· Barrels are handled manually using manpower and makeshift lifting aids.
· Manual methods increase injury risk and loading time.
· No standardised vehicle-mounted barrel handling solution exists.

Technology Domains

· Mechanical and Hydraulic Systems
· Automotive Power Take-Off
· Integration
· Structural Design and Load Handling
Vehicle Integration and Safety Systems

Application / Use Case

· The system shall support loading and unloading of filled barrels.
· The swivel arm shall be used during field logistics and routine replenishment tasks.
· The solution shall be functional in extreme cold/hot/humid climate.

Project Outcome

· A validated, retrofittable barrel handling swivel arm for Army vehicles.
· The system shall be ready for induction and fleet-wide retrofitting.

Expectation from Prototype

· The swivel arm shall provide smooth, controlled and safe lifting operations.
· The system shall demonstrate reliable operation using vehicle engine power.
· The design shall allow quick deployment and stowage during operations.

Testing / Certification

· Load testing and endurance trials
· Vehicle integration and operational trials
· User validation by the Indian Army

Potential Market

· Indian Army and other Armed Forces.
· Paramilitary and government logistics organisations.
· Huge civilian logistics applications.

Business Case

· High potential for bulk procurement across
ALS and 2.5 Ton fleets.
· Repeat orders expected due to retrofitting across existing vehicles.
· Export potential exists for similar military vehicle fleets.
