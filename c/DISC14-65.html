DISC 14 IAF DISC14-65 PS 65

Problem Statement

1. As part of green energy drive, large number of solar panels are either installed or planned to be installed at various defence installations. These solar panels have bright reflective surfaces, giving away the location of the installation from large distances.
2. The efficiency of solar panels depends upon the intensity of incident solar radiation. Thus, in present form no existing camouflage measures (painting, camouflage nets etc.) can be applied.
3. Application of any kind of available paint over the solar panel surface blocks/ significantly reduces the efficiency of solar panel.
4. The available camouflage nets, which are designed to provide camouflage cover to the installations, block the lR solar radiation and hamper the solar panel functioning. Additionally, the camouflage nets/ Multi Spectral Camouflage Nets (MSCN) are designed to even block lR radiation, thereby making them unsuitable for covering the solar panels.

Challenge Brief / Definition

To design and develop Camouflage solution for solar panels installed at Defence Installations which should include the following: -
1. Create a non-reflective disruptive pattern for solar panels and its associated accessories/ equipment.
2. The repair and maintenance must be possible in case of damage.
3. The solution should be modular to permit small scale to large scale deployment.
4. Should be easily deployable and removable over solar panels
5. Capability to camouflage existing/ upcoming solar panels.
6. Should be weather tolerant so as to be used in different weathers.
7. Should have capability of selecting required disruptive pattern, so as to merge with surrounding (covering topography along length and breadth of India).
8. Should be durable and have less maintenance cost.
9. Easily implementable/ deployable over solar panels of variable dimensions.
