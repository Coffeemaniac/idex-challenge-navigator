Swarm Technology Based Heavy Payload Logistics Drones With 200 Kg Load Capacity

DISC 14 Indian Army DISC14-4 PS 4

Problem Statement

· Last-mile delivery of supplies to forward posts, isolated detachments and dynamic operational forces remains a significant challenge due to terrain constraints, weather conditions, hostile action and limited road or air accessibility.
· Single-drone logistics solutions suffer from limitations of payload, range, survivability and single-point failure, reducing reliability in contested or adverse environments.
· There is a requirement for an indigenous swarm-based unmanned aerial logistics system capable of resilient and scalable last-mile delivery operations.

Challenge Brief / Definition

· The challenge is to design, develop and demonstrate a swarm drone-based logistics system for autonomous or semi-autonomous last-mile delivery of military supplies.
· The system shall consist of multiple cooperative unmanned aerial vehicles capable of coordinated flight, task sharing, dynamic re-routing and mission continuation in the event of individual drone loss.
· The solution shall be rugged, secure, scalable and suitable for deployment in varied operational environments including high altitude, jungle, desert and island terrain.

Existing Solution

· Predominantly single-platform systems with limited payload and operational resilience.
· Manned Aerial Resupply remains costly, risky and not optimised for small, frequent last-mile deliveries.
· Existing solutions lack redundancy, survivability and scalability in contested environments.

Technology Domains

· Swarm Intelligence and Distributed AI
· Autonomous UAV Systems
· Secure Mesh Communication Networks
· Navigation, Obstacle Avoidance and Collision
Prevention
· Payload Handling and Precision Delivery Systems

Application / Use Case

· Swarm drones shall deliver rations, FOL, ammunition, medical stores, and spares to forward posts with each drone capable of lifting 200 kgs of Composite load with range 30 Km.
· The system shall support operations in inaccessible or high-risk operational areas through landing operations in plains and desert terrai006E.
· Multiple locations shall be resupplied simultaneously during operational surges or emergencies.
· The solution shall support routine and time-critical logistics missions.

Project Outcome

· A validated swarm drone system for reliable last-mile logistics delivery.
· The solution shall be suitable for limited induction and future scaling.

Expectation from Prototype

· Self-healing behaviour after individual drone failure.
· Secure, resilient communications throughout the mission.
· Scalability in swarm size, payload, and range.

Testing / Certification

· UAV flight performance and swarm coordination trials.
· Communication security and resilience validation.
· User trials and operational evaluation by Indian Army.

Potential Market

· Indian Armed Forces and paramilitary organisations.
· Export potential to friendly foreign militaries.
· Select humanitarian and disaster-relief applications.

Business Case

· High potential for bulk induction and repeat procurement.
· Growing demand expected with expansion of unmanned logistics doctrine.
· Export opportunities exist due to global interest in swarm logistics.
