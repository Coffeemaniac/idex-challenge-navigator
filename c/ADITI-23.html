DEVELOPMENT OF HIGH-EFFICIENCY, LIGHTWEIGHT, SPACE-GRADE SILICON–PEROVSKITE TANDEM SOLAR PANELS (150 W CLASS) FOR SMALL SATELLITES (~200 KG CLASS)

Open in full navigator → Browse all 209 challenges
ADITI 4.0 Defense Space Agency ADITI-23 PS 23

Problem Statement

Indian small satellite missions require high power density, lightweight, cost-effective solar panels. Current silicon-based panels are limited in areal efficiency and specific power. There is a need to develop indigenous space- grade Silicon–Perovskite tandem solar panels (TRL-8) with ≥22% AM0 efficiency and ≥80 W/kg specific power for 200 kg class satellites.

Challenge Brief / Definition

The challenge seeks development of:
• Space-grade thin Silicon solar cells (60– 80 μm)
• Space-grade Perovskite cells
• Silicon–Perovskite tandem cells
• 150 W rigid solar panel (mandatory)
• Flexible rollout panel (desirable)
• Demonstration to TRL-8 (ground qualification), TRL-9 (flight optional)
Performance targets:
• ≥22% efficiency at AM0 (28°C)
• ≥200 W/m2 areal power density (BOL)
• ≥80 W/kg specific power
• Radiation tolerant encapsulation
• Thermo-vac capability −120°C to +120°C
• Deployment demonstration (1g or 0g)

Existing Solution

(if Global:
· Lab-scale perovskite tandem ~30% at AM1.5
Limitations:
· GaAs: Expensive, import dependent
· No indigenous TRL-8 tandem solution available

 ISR small satellites

Project Outcome

Development of a TRL-8 qualified, 150 W class Silicon–Perovskite tandem solar panel for ~200 kg small satellites, achieving ≥22% efficiency under AM0 conditions. The system shall deliver ≥200
W/m2 areal power density and ≥80 W/kg specific power, with full thermovac, vibration, acoustic and radiation qualification. The outcome will be a space- ready, integration-compatible, indigenous solar power solution, enabling higher payload capability and reduced import dependence.

Expectation from Prototype

• Enable modular power augmentation
• Indigenous supply chain establishment

Testing / Certification

• AM0 performance test
• Electroluminescence
• Thermal shock: −100°C to +100°C (500 cycles)
• Thermo-vac: 1×10 − 6 torr
• Vibration & acoustic (PSLV/ASLV loads)
• Insulation resistance tests
• LAPSS validation
