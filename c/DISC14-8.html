DISC 14 Indian Army DISC14-8 PS 8

Problem Statement

Helicopter-Mounted Anti-Drone System for helicopter of IA
(Army Aviation)

Challenge Brief / Definition

· Indian Army Aviation helicopters (Attack, Utility and
Reconnaissance roles) are vulnerable to emerging small-
Unmanned Aerial Systems (UAS) threats due to the following reasons:-
· Increasing employment of drones by adversaries.
· Modern drone tactics include silent ambush modes and
Kamikaze style attacks.
· No onboard system providing early warning of drones in vicinity.
· Absence of drone detection sensors capable of identifying incoming FPV drones, commercial drones and capability to jam or destroy the drones.

Existing Solution

(if Ground based C-UAS available. Imported airborne EW dependent.

Technology Domains

RF Spectrum Monitoring, AI/ML Signal Classification,
Embedded Systems, Directional RF/ GNSS Jamming,
Avionics Integration.
Integration of the system on Indian Army Aviation

Application / Use Case

Helicopter Mounted Anti Drone Sys will be utilised on all type of R&O, Utility, Attack Helicopter for detection & jamming of Drones during conduct of flying operations, high altitude missions, logistics missions, armed LCH missions, hover/ low-speed regimes.

Project Outcome

Flight validated lightweight C-UAS meeting PSQR compliance.

Expectation from Prototype

helicopters will improve survivability, provide early warning, reduce vulnerability and enhance flight safety and mission efficiency.
· The anti-drone system should be based on a passive sensor and/ or miniaturized active sensor to detect and jam/ destroy the drones.
· The anti-drone system should be preferably integrated with the helicopter avionics for displaying the threats via on- board Multi-function Displays.
· The anti-drone jamming should not affect any on-board radio systems.

Testing / Certification

· Helicopter Airworthiness and integration to be certified by
CEMILAC (RCMA).
· Counter drone payload performance test to be carried out at as per JSS 55555 Test Facilities at Defence test ranges vis- à-vis eval by evaluation agency as per Qrs/GSQRs.
· Safety EMI/EMC and Environmental Qualification will be undertaken by ACE, Mhow as per MIL-STD-810G standards.
· Cyber security, software and Data to be certified by Defence
CERT/ ACG.
· QA and Acceptance will be done by DGAQA (AHSP).

Potential Market

· Helicopter mounted anti drone systems provides significant tactical flexibility as they can be quickly repositioned and deployed with advancing ground forces. They are vital for defending against drone swarms and low-flying threats that traditional static defenses may miss.

Business Case

Bulk orders may be planned later.
