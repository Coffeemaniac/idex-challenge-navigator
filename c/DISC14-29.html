Design and Development ship borne Autonomous Aerial Vehicle (AAV) to deploy, recover and re-deploy AS sensor(s) / payload (s) from platforms at sea

DISC 14 Indian Navy DISC14-29 PS 29

Problem Statement

Underwater surveillance & monitoring rely on distributed sensor network for continuous and effective operations. Deployment, recovery and re-deployment of underwater sensors is time consuming and resource intensive. Thus, there is a critical need for a ship based
AAV which is capable of deploying, recovery and re-deploying IN defined portable AS sensor(s)/ payload from platform at sea.

Challenge Brief / Definition

The objective is to design and develop a ship based AAV that can transport multiple AS sensors, deploy them at pre-defined or dynamically assigned locations with positional accuracy, recover sensors after mission completion and re-deploy them as operational requirement evolve. The vehicle must be capable of operate at high seas with reliable precision navigation and ensure safe handling of sensitive sensor payloads

Existing Solution

(if Manual deployment from ships, s/m & aircrafts.

Technology Domains

· Unmanned/Autonomous Systems
· Robotics and intelligent control
· Robotic manipulation and payload handling
· Use of advanced materials for marine environment
· Navigation and localization technologies such as GNSS and vision based navigation
· Artificial intelligence and mission planning algorithms
· Swarm coordination including longer endurance

Application / Use Case

AAV is envisaged to be used for following:-
· Landing/ take off from platforms at sea.
· Deploy IN defined portable AS sensor(s)/ payload (s) from platform at sea to specified area.
· Recover the sensor post mission completion onboard platform.
· Re-deploy as required

Project Outcome

AAV with a mechanism to handle/ transport/ deploy/ recover a cylindrical weight not exceeding 50 Kgs in area of operations at sea

Expectation from Prototype

· Autonomous capability with a minimum endurance of 08 hr
· Precise handling of the payload and efficient mission execution
· Technology demonstrator capable of scalable operational deployment
