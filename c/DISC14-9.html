Automatic Snow Chain System for Army “B” Vehicles

Open in full navigator → Browse all 209 challenges
DISC 14 Indian Army DISC14-9 PS 9

Problem Statement

· Indian Army vehicles operate extensively in snow-bound and icy regions where vehicle mobility is severely restricted due to reduced traction, verglas formation and heavy snowfall.
· Presently, snow chains are either manually installed or not available on all vehicle types, leading to significant delays, increased manpower effort, safety hazards to personnel, and reduced operational mobility.
· There is a requirement for an automatic or rapidly deployable snow chain system compatible across multiple
Army “B” vehicle platforms to ensure assured mobility in snow and icy conditions without manual intervention.

Challenge Brief / Definition

· The challenge is to design, develop and demonstrate an indigenous automatic snow chain system that can be fitted to a wide range of Army “B” vehicles.
· The system shall allow 'Electronic On-Demand
Engagement and Disengagement' of Snow Chains from within the Driver's cabin, without requiring the crew to dismount in adverse weather conditions.
· The solution shall be rugged, reliable, easy to maintain and capable of functioning effectively in extreme cold, snow and icy terrains.

Existing Solution

· Manual snow chains are presently used on select vehicles in limited areas.
· These require vehicle stoppage, crew exposure to extreme weather, and significant time for installation and removal.
· No standardised, automatic or universal snow chain system is currently available across the Army “B” vehicle fleet.

Technology Domains

· Mechanical and Electromechanical Systems
· Automotive Engineering
· Cold Weather Materials and Design
· Actuation and Control Systems
· Vehicle Integration and Safety Systems

Application / Use Case

· The automatic snow chain system shall be applicable to all Army 'B' vehicles including ALS Trucks, 2.5 Ton vehicles, Gypsy, Safari, Scorpio, Force Light Strike
Vehicle (LSV), Water Bowsers and Ambulances.
· The system shall support uninterrupted vehicle movement in snow-bound areas, icy roads and high-altitude regions during routine operations, convoys and emergency missions.
· The solution shall be particularly useful for logistics convoys, casualty evacuation, water supply and emergency response operations where delays can have serious operational consequences.
· The system shall enhance mobility during training exercises and operational deployments in winter conditions.

Project Outcome

· A validated automatic snow chain system compatible with a wide range of Army “B” vehicles.
· The solution shall be ready for induction and retrofitting across existing and future vehicle fleets.
