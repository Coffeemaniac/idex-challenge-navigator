Modular Soft-Kill & Hard-Kill Integrated Counter-Unmanned Aerial System (C-UAS) Kit for Regt of Arty

Open in full navigator → Browse all 209 challenges
DISC 14 Indian Army DISC14-17 PS 17

Problem Statement

Design and develop an indigenous Modular Soft-Kill &
Hard-Kill Integrated C-UAS Kit for Artillery guns, Long
Range Vectors (LRVs) &
SATA assets, with capability to detect, track and neutralise
Nano, micro and small UAS including swarms through selectable soft-kill and/ or hard-kill mechanisms, with open architecture, high indigenous content and rapid field deployability.

Challenge Brief / Definition

There is an increasing threat from nano, micro and small- unmanned aerial systems being employed for surveillance, targeting, payload delivery and swarm attacks against military forces and critical assets like guns, artillery ammunition dumps and LRVs. Existing C-UAS solutions are largely single-mode (only soft-kill or only hard-kill), platform-specific, expensive and limited in indigenous content. A need exists for an indigenous, modular C-UAS kit capable of detecting, tracking and neutralising hostile
UAS using selectable soft-kill (RF/GNSS jamming) and hard-kill (kinetic / net /interceptor) mechanisms. The system should be man-portable, vehicle-mountable, battery powered and configurable through plug-and-play modules.
· Imported soft-kill jammers with limited configurability.
· Hard-kill gun-based or missile-based solutions with high cost.
· Platform-centric integrated systems with low portability.
Limitations
· Lack modularity and rapid reconfiguration.
· Have limited indigenous content.
· High cost and difficult to scale.
There is no lightweight, integrated, indigenous plug-and- play
C-UAS kit providing both soft-kill and hard-kill capabilities in a single architecture.

Technology Domains

Broad Technical Requirements (Indicative).
· Multi-band jammer covering 400 MHz to 6 GHz,
GNSS
L1/ L2 and other frequency spectrum being used by enemy UAS.
· Directional and/or Omni antenna options.
· Open architecture with standard interfaces.
· Encrypted data links.
· Weight (man-portable configuration): ≤ 25 kg.
· Setup time: ≤ 5 minutes.
The System Should.
· Detect and track UAS within 3–5 km range.
· Provide RF and GNSS jamming (soft-kill).
· Provide optional hard-kill effector (net launcher/ micro interceptor/ kinetic solution).
· Allow modular plug-and-play configuration, which can be easily installed by gun crews.
· Be man-portable and vehicle mountable.
· Operate on battery power with minimum 4 hours endurance.
· Provide central control through handheld controller or tablet
Support standalone and networked operation.

Application / Use Case

/ · Various types of Artillery units to include, guns, LRV &
SATA assets.
· Border and forward posts.
· Air bases and naval installations.
· Ammunition depots and logistics nodes.
· High-value government facilities.
· Urban counter-terror operations.
· Air defence units.
The system must be operable in desert, plains, mountains, high altitude, coastal and urban environments, under day/night and adverse weather conditions.

Project Outcome

· A production ready modular system that transforms and positions India as a leader in advanced CUAS technology.
· Functional prototype C-UAS kits, which are easy to install.
· Documentation to include technical manuals, Interface
Control Documents, safety cases, support and training packages.
· Clear roadmap for productisation, large scale induction and future technology insertion like improved AI.

Expectation from Prototype

· Should give out product improvement roadmap for future variants.

Testing / Certification

/ 1. EMI/EMC compliance.
2. Environmental testing.
3. Safety and reliability trials.
4. User field evaluation.
· Accuracy Trials.
· Target Effectiveness.
· Multi-role Performance.
· Operational Scenario Testing: Day/night, weather conditions.
· 5. Cybersecurity evaluation and certification for communication links and software components.

Potential Market

· Artillery Regiments across Indian Army to include towed and self-propelled guns, MBRL units, SATA units.
Potential extension of requirement to Air Defence,
Armoured Regiments, Para military forces and critical infrastructure protection.
· Central Armed Police Forces.
· State Police Forces.
· Airports and critical infrastructure.
· Export to Friendly Foreign Nations.

Business Case

High volume demand across multiple agencies, low production cost through COTS components and strong export potential make the solution commercially viable.
