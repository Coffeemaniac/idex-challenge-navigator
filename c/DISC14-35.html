Rearming by Drone (REARM-D) at Sea

DISC 14 Indian Navy DISC14-35 PS 35

Problem Statement

Navies across the globe are facing omnipresent aerial threats like swarm of low cost drones and missiles, which entails faster depletion of Surface to Air Missiles (SAM) onboard and thus necessitates ability of ‘Re-Arming at Sea’. The concept of ‘Rearming by Drone at Sea’ refers to capability of replenishing the Surface to Air Missiles
(SAMs) in ship’s VLU cells using a Multi-Rotor Drone.

Challenge Brief / Definition

Transferring of Missile Canister
3.1 The proposed Multi rotor drone (with IC Engine) shall transfer missile canister from supply ship to a receiving ship at a precise location.
3.2 Drone shall have a gyro stabilised platform to minimize payload swing during takeoff. Loading of Missile Canister to VLU Cell
3.3 Hovering and loading of missile canister in VLU cell shall be a guided high precision operation. Automatic movement cut-off feature shall be embedded in drone to restrict any movement post completion of alignment over VLU cell.
3.4 Suitable Loading Mechanism (portable & removable) shall be provisioned on designated VLU Cell for vertically loading of the missile canister.
3.5 Suitable provisioned for variable speeds finalized during
Winch Mechanism shall be lowering of missile canister at inside
VLU (Specifications to be design and trial phase). Endurance of
Drone
3.6 Robust IC Engine shall sustain prolonged station keeping and flights in various conditions such as controlled ship speeds, wind directions and weather patterns.
3.7 Minimum Endurance ≥ 120 min with payload carrying capacity
≥ 900 Kgs.
